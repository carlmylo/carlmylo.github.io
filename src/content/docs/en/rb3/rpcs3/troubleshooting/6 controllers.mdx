<details>
<summary>My game locks up when naming things or inviting people.</summary>
This varies from controller to controller.
* Try moving the whammy or effects switch on your guitar.
* Reconnect your controller.
* Open the Gamepad Configuration menu and close it.
* Enable **"Keep Pads Connected"** as mentioned in [[the I/O section of Custom Configuration]](https://rb3pc.milohax.org/custom_config_io).
* Disable the Native Interface as mentioned in [[the Emulator section of Custom Configuration]](https://rb3pc.milohax.org/custom_config_emu).
* Use a standard [[gamepad]](https://rb3pc.milohax.org/ctrls#gamepads) or keyboard for any sort of character customization or naming.
</details>


<details>
<summary>[Guitar Hero guitars] Solos have auto-strum enabled.</summary>
Unbind L2 from your guitar in your gamepad configuration by right clicking it.  
![A GIF of a RPCS3's Gamepad Configuration. L2 button is being unbound.](https://rb3pc.milohax.org/images/instruments/xtra/gtrs/solol2.gif "L2 Unbound")
</details>

<details>
<summary>My PS3 Rock Band instrument controller shows up as two controllers.</summary>
You did [[controller configuration]](https://rb3pc.milohax.org/ctrls) for a PS3 Rock Band controller, which usually isn’t needed due to passthrough. Just unbind the controller and it should be fine.
</details>

<details>
<summary>[MIDI Drums] My E-Kit doesn't work.</summary>
* Windows doesn’t let programs share MIDI devices. If you have anything else open that is using the port (including MIDI Monitor), close it before starting Rock Band 3 in RPCS3.
* Make sure your E-Kit is mapped correctly and on the right channel (Channel 10). Refer to [[the MIDI Drums page]](https://rb3pc.milohax.org/ctrls_drums_midi) for more help.
</details>

<details>
<summary>[Pro Drums] Hitting two cymbals registers as a tom.</summary>
This is an infamous Rock Band 3 bug called the “double cymbal glitch” and plagues all versions of Rock Band 3, even console versions. You can try to slightly [[flam]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Drum_rudiment#Flam) the two inputs to get around this.
</details>

<details>
<summary>[MIDI Keys] My Keyboard doesn't work.</summary>
* Windows doesn’t let programs share MIDI devices. If you have anything else open that is using the port (including MIDI Monitor), close it before starting Rock Band 3 in RPCS3.
* Make sure your keyboard the right channel (Channel 1). Refer to [[the MIDI Keyboard page]](https://rb3pc.milohax.org/ctrls_keys_midi) for more help.
* If you're on a Rock Band 3 keyboard in MIDI mode, you may have activated drum mode by pressing D-Pad Up. Press D-Pad Up again to toggle it off.
</details>

<details>
<summary>[Pro Guitar] My guitar doesn't work.</summary>
* Pro Guitars require a MIDI interface with SYSEX capabilities. Refer to [[your Pro Guitar's page]](https://rb3pc.milohax.org/ctrls_protars) to see which interfaces are confirmed to work.
* Windows doesn’t let programs share MIDI devices. If you have anything else open that is using the port (including MIDI Monitor), close it before starting Rock Band 3 in RPCS3.
</details>

<details>
<summary>I cannot use Automatic Calibration in System Settings.</summary>
Automatic Calibration only works for PS3 Rock Band guitar/bass and Fender Mustang Pro Guitar controllers with passthrough.
</details>
